PRD: Personal Finance AI Agent
Product Name
Money Sentinel
Alternative names: Spendwise Agent, Personal CFO, Finance Copilot, Pocket Auditor
One-Liner
An AI personal finance agent that tracks spending, subscriptions, banking, and investments across accounts, then provides twice-weekly check-ins with personalized feedback and actionable financial guardrails.
Problem
People’s financial lives are fragmented across checking accounts, credit cards, brokerages, subscriptions, and payment apps. Existing tools show dashboards, but users still have to interpret the data themselves.
Users need an agent that can:
notice spending patterns
detect waste
connect spending with investment and savings goals
explain what changed
recommend next actions
check in regularly without needing to be asked
Target User
Young professionals, students, and early-career earners who use multiple financial platforms:
Chase, Capital One, AmEx, Bank of America, etc.
Robinhood or other brokerages
subscriptions like Netflix, Spotify, Notion, Figma, ChatGPT
food delivery, groceries, rideshare, travel, shopping
Core Product Thesis
Most finance apps are dashboards. This should feel like an agent watching over your financial life.
The differentiator:
“You don’t just track money. You get reviewed twice a week by an AI that knows your patterns, goals, subscriptions, and investments.”

Feasibility In 2 Hours
Feasible
Build a polished Vite/React demo with:
mocked connected accounts
dashboard overview
spending breakdown
transaction feed
subscription tracker
investment snapshot
AI-generated twice-weekly check-in cards
user-adjustable goals/budgets
recommendations based on sample data
“agent findings” panel
Not Feasible
In 2 hours, do not attempt:
real Plaid integration
real Robinhood integration
OAuth bank connections
scheduled email/SMS/push notification backend
production-grade AI memory
secure secrets infrastructure
real financial advice workflows
transaction categorization model from scratch
Best Hackathon Approach
Use mock data but design the system as if integrations exist.
Example:
Connected accounts:
- Chase Checking
- Chase Freedom Unlimited
- Robinhood
- AmEx Gold
- Spotify
- Netflix
- DoorDash
Then clearly say in the pitch:
“For the demo, we simulate account aggregation. In production, this would connect through Plaid, brokerage APIs, and subscription detection from transaction streams.”

MVP Scope
1. Dashboard
The first screen should show the user’s financial state at a glance.
Key cards:
Total cash
Credit card balance
Spending this week
Subscriptions this month
Investment portfolio value
Safe-to-spend amount
AI risk level
Example:
Cash: $4,820
Card balance: $1,240
Investments: $12,600
Subscriptions: $187/mo
Safe to spend this week: $312
Agent status: Watch mode
2. Twice-Weekly AI Check-In
This is the signature feature.
The check-in should summarize:
what changed since last check-in
where the user overspent
upcoming bills/subscriptions
investment changes
one specific action to take
Example:
Tuesday Check-In

You spent $218 on dining this week, 43% above your normal pace.
Your Robinhood portfolio is up $320, mostly from NVDA and VOO.
Three subscriptions renew before Friday: Spotify, Notion, and Netflix.

Recommended action:
Pause DoorDash this week and move $75 to savings to stay on track for your August goal.
3. Spending Dashboard
Break down spending by category:
groceries
dining
transport
subscriptions
shopping
rent/utilities
investing transfers
Show weekly and monthly comparisons.
Important: make this feel operational, not decorative. Users should be able to quickly see what category needs attention.
4. Subscription Monitor
Detect recurring charges from transaction data.
Each subscription should show:
merchant
monthly cost
renewal date
usage/status
AI recommendation
Example:
Netflix - $22.99/mo - Keep
Spotify - $11.99/mo - Keep
Figma - $15/mo - Review
Peacock - $7.99/mo - Cancel candidate
5. Investment Snapshot
Keep this lightweight. Do not make stock recommendations.
Show:
portfolio value
weekly change
largest holdings
concentration risk
recent movement explanation
Example:
Your portfolio is up 2.8% this week.
Most of the move came from NVDA and VOO.
AI exposure is now 41% of your portfolio, above your 30% target.
6. Goals And Guardrails
Users can tailor the agent by setting:
weekly food budget
max subscriptions budget
emergency fund goal
investing goal
max single-stock concentration
safe-to-spend target
This makes the AI feel personal instead of generic.
Recommended Demo Screens
For 2 hours, build these four screens:
Overview
Main dashboard with balances, spending, subscriptions, investments, and AI summary.

AI Check-In
Twice-weekly report with findings and recommended actions.

Spending
Category charts, transactions, and budget progress.

Rules / Goals
User-defined financial guardrails.

AI Behavior
The AI should not say “buy this stock” or “sell this stock.”
It should say:
“your portfolio is concentrated”
“this purchase affects your goal”
“this subscription looks unused”
“your grocery spending is trending high”
“you can safely spend X before Friday”
“you are on/off track”
Differentiators
1. Twice-Weekly Financial Review
Most apps wait for the user to open them. This agent proactively checks in.
2. Whole-Life Context
It connects spending, subscriptions, banking, and investing in one place.
3. Financial Guardrails
Users define rules, and the agent monitors against them.
4. Safe-To-Spend Number
A simple number users can act on.
5. Agent Mode
Position the product as an agent that watches and learns, not just a dashboard.

Success Metrics
For demo:
User understands financial state in under 10 seconds
AI check-in produces at least 3 useful insights
User can adjust at least 2 goals/guardrails
App clearly shows spending, subscriptions, and investments together
For production:
weekly active users
check-in open rate
subscriptions canceled
spending reduction by category
savings goal progress
user trust score
recommendation acceptance rate
Risks
Data Access
Bank and brokerage integrations require secure providers like Plaid, MX, Finicity, or direct APIs.
Privacy
Financial data is sensitive. The production product needs encryption, clear data controls, and minimal data exposure to AI models.
Compliance
Investment advice is regulated. Avoid explicit buy/sell/hold recommendations unless operating through the right legal framework.
Notification Fatigue
Twice-weekly check-ins should be concise and useful. Too many alerts will feel annoying.
Recommended 2-Hour Build Plan
0-20 min
Create mock financial dataset: accounts, transactions, subscriptions, investments, goals.

20-60 min
Build overview dashboard and spending breakdown.

60-90 min
Build AI check-in screen with generated insights from the mock data.

90-110 min
Build goals/guardrails editor.

110-120 min
Polish UI, run build, prepare demo script.

Demo Narrative
Use this story:
“Meet Alex. Alex uses Chase, Robinhood, several credit cards, and has too many subscriptions. Instead of making Alex inspect five apps, Money Sentinel reviews everything twice a week and explains what changed, what matters, and what to do next.”

Then show:
Dashboard: full financial picture
Check-in: AI explains spending/investing changes
Subscription monitor: finds waste
Goals: user adjusts guardrails
Updated recommendation: AI adapts
Recommendation
Build the MVP as a personal finance agent with check-ins and guardrails, not as a generic budgeting app.
The best positioning is:
An AI agent that reviews your whole financial life twice a week, catches waste, tracks goals, and tells you what to do next across spending, subscriptions, banking, and investing.

That is absolutely demoable in 2 hours with mocked data, and it gives you a strong path toward a real product later.
